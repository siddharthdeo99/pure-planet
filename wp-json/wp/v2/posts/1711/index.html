Ingredients<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

for\u00a012\u00a0cookies<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n
  • \u00bd cup granulated sugar\u00a0(100 g)<\/li>\n
  • \u00be cup brown sugar\u00a0(165\u00a0g), packed<\/li>\n
  • 1 teaspoon\u00a0salt<\/li>\n
  • \u00bd cup unsalted butter\u00a0(115\u00a0g), melted<\/li>\n
  • 1\u00a0egg<\/li>\n
  • 1 teaspoon\u00a0vanilla extract<\/li>\n
  • 1 \u00bc cups all-purpose flour\u00a0(155\u00a0g)<\/li>\n
  • \u00bd teaspoon\u00a0baking soda<\/li>\n
  • 110g\u00a0milk or semi-sweet chocolate chunks<\/li>\n
  • 110g\u00a0dark chocolate chunk, or your preference<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    Preparation<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
      \n
    1. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugars, salt, and butter until a paste forms with no lumps.<\/li>\n
    2. Whisk in the egg and vanilla, beating until light ribbons fall off the whisk and remain for a short while before falling back into the mixture.<\/li>\n
    3. Sift in the flour and baking soda, then fold the mixture with a spatula (Be careful not to overmix, which would cause the gluten in the flour to toughen resulting in cakier cookies).<\/li>\n
    4. Fold in the chocolate chunks, then chill the dough for at least 30 minutes. For a more intense toffee-like flavour and deeper colour, chill the dough overnight. The longer the dough rests, the more complex its flavour will be.<\/li>\n
    5. Preheat oven to 180\u00b0C. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.<\/li>\n
    6. Scoop the dough with an ice-cream scoop onto a parchment paper-lined baking sheet, leaving at least 10 cm of space between cookies and 5 cm of space from the edges of the pan so that the cookies can spread evenly.<\/li>\n
    7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges have started to barely brown.<\/li>\n
    8. Cool completely before serving.<\/li>\n
