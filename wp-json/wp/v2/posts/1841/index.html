While the product descriptions of these wipes may boast about gentle cleansing towelettes that whisk away dirt and makeup, they don’t mention the fact that these seemingly harmless wipes are wreaking havoc on our earth.<\/strong> Used for a minute and tossed away, these wipes end up in landfill or even worse our waterways<\/strong>.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

It's time to get real about the environmental impacts of makeup remover wipes!<\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

It is estimated that makeup remover wipes are responsible for about 10 per cent<\/strong> of the wastewater stream, while toilet paper only makes up 6 per cent. There are 3.4 billion kilograms of\u00a0wipes thrown\u00a0into the landfill\u00a0each year<\/strong>, which add up to 1.3 billion\u00a0wipes tossed each\u00a0day<\/strong>. That’s 20 million\u00a0makeup wipes\u00a0a day being\u00a0thrown\u00a0into the bin! And if you thought their damage stops there, you’re sadly mistaken.<\/p>

Makeup remover wipes are actually made using plastic fibres<\/strong> \ud83e\udd2f so when flushed or thrown away, they can leak tiny micro-plastics into our waterways and oceans<\/strong>. Scientists have already found these micro-plastics in over 140 ocean species, including the ones we eat.<\/p>

It’s not just makeup remover wipes that are bad news either! Hygiene wipes, baby wipes and antibacterial wipes ALL have harmful impacts on the environment<\/strong>, especially supposed ‘flushable wipes’<\/strong> which have a huge impact on sewage systems and wastewater<\/strong> treatment plants not to mention the pipes they clog up and break down.<\/p>

It’s never been more important for us to eliminate our dependence<\/strong> on<\/strong> these disposable products<\/strong> and find more efficient, eco-friendly ways to live!<\/p>
