With Christmas around the corner, we’ve all started preparing for the festive season. Sadly, Christmas can come with a significant price on the environment<\/strong>. From the gifts<\/strong> we buy, wrapping paper<\/strong> we use to the food<\/strong> we eat, every aspect has an impact<\/strong>. That’s why we’ve put together this helpful list of 4 things you can do to make your Christmas more environmentally friendly.\u00a0<\/strong><\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t

Tip 1: Rethink Your Decorations<\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

There’s nothing quite as exciting as decking your home out with Christmas lights and decorations. However, this year why not consider taking a more natural approach to your decor<\/strong>? When you’re buying new decorations consider what they are made of<\/strong> and whether you could use more natural materials to create the same effect?<\/strong> For example, eco-friendly alternatives to fairy lights<\/strong> include solar-powered or LED lights<\/strong>.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t

Tip 4: Think About What You're Buying This Year\n\n<\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Christmas is a time for giving – but it doesn’t always need to include buying new things<\/strong>. Why not try and give eco-friendly gifts instead<\/strong>? Try shopping in second-hand stores like The Bower Reuse & Repair Centre<\/strong> or using online platforms like Marketplace<\/strong> and Gumtree<\/strong> to do your Christmas shopping.<\/p>

Try swapping out physical gifts with experiences.<\/strong> Experiences are a unique gift that will leave the receiver with a lifetime of memories<\/strong>. They tend to be more social<\/strong> and make us feel happier for longer<\/strong>. Gifting something like a meal out, or a weekend away is the perfect way to make your friends or family feel special without giving them a physical gift.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t

Tip 2: Rethink Your Wrapping Paper<\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Who doesn’t love getting a present wrapped in beautifully decorated paper? It’s exciting and adds that extra special touch! But this year why not try something different and use recycled or biodegradable<\/strong> wrapping paper instead.<\/p>

Some gifts don’t even need wrapping – like giftcards and experiences.<\/strong> Reduce where you can, and reconsider where you can’t. If you do need to buy wrapping paper, go for an uncoated paper that can be recycled afterwards.<\/strong><\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t

Tip 3: Rethink Your Food Choices<\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

The food you eat at Christmas is also one of the main reasons for its high eco-footprint. From cooking, to washing up and even cleaning appliances used in preparation – every aspect impacts your carbon footprint<\/strong> this time of year. The good news is, that there are lots of delicious festive recipes<\/strong> that don’t require any special ingredients. Try replacing meat with vegetarian alternatives<\/strong>, and pineapples or strawberries (which have a high water consumption) with eco-friendly fruit like figs<\/strong>, which are great for the environment and delicious for us.<\/p>

Is fish a Christmas staple in your home?<\/strong> Not to worry. While overfishing is a massive problem<\/strong> in the world, it is more evident in countries with poor regulations and limits. If you want to enjoy eco-friendly fish<\/strong> this festive season, then buy from sustainable sellers<\/strong>, who know exactly where their fish is coming from. You can also go straight to the source and buy from the people who caught it at fish markets<\/strong>! Our biggest tip is to stick to species that are in season and local!<\/strong><\/p>

When it comes to serving your Christmas meal, say no to plastic and opt for reusable plates and cutlery.<\/strong> This might make for a bigger cleanup but the benefits far outway the risks in the long run.<\/p>

There are many ways to reduce your impact this festive season. By simply following our four tips<\/strong>, you’ll be able to celebrate a more sustainable Christmas<\/strong> with your family and friends without sacrificing any of the fun<\/strong>! We hope you found our list helpful and we wish you an eco-friendly Christmas this year!<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/section>\n\t\t\t\t
